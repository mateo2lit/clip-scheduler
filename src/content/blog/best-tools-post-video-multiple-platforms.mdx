Most "social media scheduling" tools are built for marketing teams posting text updates and images. Video creators have different needs: large file uploads, platform-specific format toggles, Shorts vs. regular YouTube video distinctions, clip import from Twitch/Kick, TikTok's duet/stitch permissions, Instagram Reels vs. Stories.

This comparison focuses specifically on tools for **video creators** — not general social media managers.

## What to Look for in a Multi-Platform Video Tool

Before the comparison, the key criteria for video-focused cross-posting:

1. **Video upload size and format support** — most scheduling tools cap uploads at 500 MB; real video files are 1–4 GB
2. **Platform-specific settings** — YouTube needs privacy levels and Shorts toggle; TikTok needs duet/stitch/comment permissions; Instagram needs Reels vs. Stories choice
3. **Multiple accounts per platform** — if you run two YouTube channels or two TikTok accounts, you need to post to both
4. **Clip import** — for streamers, importing from Twitch or Kick without downloading is a time-saver
5. **Bluesky support** — Bluesky's video community is growing; not all tools have it yet
6. **Pricing that makes sense for a solo creator** — enterprise pricing is common in this space

### Buffer

**Best for:** Social media managers handling multiple brands

Buffer is a mature scheduling tool with a clean interface. Video support exists but isn't the primary focus — upload limits are lower than Clip Dash and Buffer doesn't have Twitch/Kick integration. It handles YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, and LinkedIn.

Strengths: clean analytics, link-in-bio tool, long track record.
Weaknesses: video upload limits (around 500 MB), no Bluesky video, no clip import, pricing scales up quickly for multiple platforms.

**Pricing:** Free tier (limited), from $6/month per channel.

---

### Hootsuite

**Best for:** Large marketing teams with multiple brands

Hootsuite is an enterprise-oriented scheduling platform. It handles video, but the interface is complex for solo creators and pricing is steep — plans start around $99/month. No Twitch/Kick integration, no Bluesky video support.

**Pricing:** From $99/month.

---

### Metricool

**Best for:** Creators who want analytics as the primary feature

Metricool does scheduling plus detailed analytics across platforms. Good video support for YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn. No Bluesky video, no Twitch/Kick import. The analytics dashboard is more detailed than most competitors.

**Pricing:** Free tier (limited), from $22/month for full features.

---

### Repurpose.io

**Best for:** Automation-first creators who want zero-touch content pipelines

Repurpose.io works differently from the others — it's automation-based. You connect a source (YouTube, TikTok, podcast RSS) and set rules to automatically mirror content to other platforms. No manual upload step needed.

The catch: you can't start with a new video and choose where it goes. Everything flows through automation rules. For streamers who want to manually review and customize each post, this is friction.

No Bluesky video, no Twitch/Kick import, no direct upload workflow.

**Pricing:** From $25/month.

---

### Later

**Best for:** Instagram-first creators

Later is strong on Instagram scheduling and visual planning. Video support exists but is secondary to its image/carousel focus. Limited for YouTube Shorts and TikTok compared to video-first tools.

**Pricing:** From $18/month.
